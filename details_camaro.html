Overview

The Chevrolet Camaro is an American muscle car icon and the primary competitor to the Ford Mustang. Since its introduction in 1966, the Camaro has represented affordable performance, aggressive styling, and American automotive passion.

It has gone through six generations, each reflecting the era's design and performance trends while maintaining the Camaro's legendary character.

Technical Specifications (SS)

  • Engine: 6.2L LT1 V8
  • Power: 455 HP @ 6,000 rpm
  • Torque: 455 lb-ft
  • Transmission: 6-speed manual
  • 0-60 mph: 4.0 seconds
  • Top Speed: 165 mph
  • Weight: 3,685 lbs
  • Year: 1966-Present

Interesting Facts

  • Over 5 million Camaros sold since 1967
  • First generation offered 80+ factory options
  • Bumblebee from Transformers is a Camaro
  • Official pace car for Indianapolis 500 multiple times
  • Production halted 2002-2009 before fifth generation
  • Appeared in over 150 movies and TV shows
